What are hyperparameters in machine learning? One of you asked me this question, so let's actually bake it wrong. Hyper parameters are the settings you choose before you actually start training your data, and you are the one who gets to decide it. Think of it like cooking. Your data is actually the recipe. The open temperature in the cook time are your hyper parameters. It's basically the same recipe, but different settings completely gives you a different result. Here are the five things that you should keep in mind for hyper parameters. First, learning great. This controls how big of a step the model takes each time it updates. Too high and the training becomes unstable or never converges too low and it crawls or gets stuck. Number two is the bad size. This is how many data points the model looks at before it updates itself. Smaller batches updates more often and often generalize better. Large batches are more stable, but can miss the detail. Number three is epox. An epox is one full pass through your entire dataset. More passes means more chances to learn too many and the model will start to memorize instead of learning. So you have to keep a balance. Number four is the network depth. Too many layers and it will overfit your training data and too few it will never capture the real pattern. Number five is the regularization. I can't say that word regularization strength. This is basically the setting that limits how complicated the model is allowed to get. A higher value basically forces the model to start and stay simple and generalize better. And each of these can completely change how your results are. In most models and data training, most people often leave everything by default or they randomly change values without even fully realizing it. One thing you need to keep in mind is that the hyperparameter will not fix bad data. They only control how well the model learns and behaves.
